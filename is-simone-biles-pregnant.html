Simone Biles Career

Simone Biles began gymnastics at the age of six on a day-care field trip, and her instructors saw her talent and encouraged her to continue. At the age of eight, she began formal training at Bannon’s Gymnastics and began working with coach Aimee Boorman.

Her career took off after she finished third all-around at the 2011 American Classic. She won first place overall at the American Classic in 2012.

Biles made her senior international debut at the 2013 American Cup and moved quickly to fame. She suffered a minor setback due to injuries in 2014, but she returned triumphantly, winning the US Classic. In the years that followed, she dominated tournaments, winning the AT&T American Cup, the City of Jesolo Trophy, and the Pacific Rim Championships.

Her extraordinary career continued in 2016 when she was named to the United States Olympic team and went on to win four gold medals in Rio de Janeiro. Biles took a vacation in 2017, but returned in 2018 with new routines. She debuted a ground-breaking Yurchenko double-pike vault in 2021.

She triumphantly returned to the U.S. Classic in 2023, winning all-around and breaking the record for most national all-around titles. Simone Biles’ career has been distinguished by unprecedented success, countless honors, and game-changing achievements to gymnastics.
