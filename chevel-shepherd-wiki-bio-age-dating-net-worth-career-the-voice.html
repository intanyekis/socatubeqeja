Chevel Shepherd’s Participation in The Voice

Chevel Shepherd appeared on the 3rd episode of the NBC show The Voice Season 15. She sang the song by The Band Perry If I Die Young for the audition.

Out of four, three judges turned around in the blind audition in favor of the teenager. Chevel was in a dilemma of choosing one judge among the three as her mentor but later selected Kelly Clarkson (American singer) as her vocal teacher.

Chevel Shepherd born on July 16, 2002, in La Plata New Mexico, U.S.A, was a music lover since she was young. She started singing at age of two and since Chevel was eight, she has been singing in town wherever she can.

Chevel grew up on country music and often doing crafts, feeding the animals, and fixing up cars with her father. Her name was given to her by her father after the name of the car Chevelle.

At just 16 years old, Chevel continuously astonishes people with her powerful voice given her age and petite stature.

Before The Voice, Shepherd sang at weddings, performed at her church and school. Shepherd also sang the national anthem for football and baseball games.
