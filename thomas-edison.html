Biography

Thomas Edison was born in Milan, OH on February 11, 1847. He invented the light bulb, the phonograph, and the motion picture camera, and became a legend for contributions to telecommunications and industry. With 1,093 U.S. patents in his name, he is considered the fourth most prolific inventor in history. He became close friends with businessmen Henry Ford and Harvey Firestone. He sold candy, newspapers, and vegetables to train passengers who were going from Port Huron to Detroit.
